80 restaurants from one Manhattan bounding box, homepage-only fingerprinting. This demonstrates pipeline architecture and detection discipline — not a production-scale account universe. Google Places/Yelp enrichment (review counts, hours, categories) is the obvious next upgrade; it needs a billed API key and isn't included here.

The GTM problem underneath the engineering problem

A voice-AI phone agent for restaurants sells against a specific, quantified pain: restaurants miss an estimated 21–43% of incoming calls during peak hours, costing roughly $100–600 per location per day in unrealized orders. Selling that story at scale — a company like Maple has stated a target of 40,000 customers, up from roughly 2,500 today — means the sales team (Maple is concurrently hiring SMB AEs and BDRs, per public job listings) needs a constant, prioritized feed of accounts, not a cold list bought once. That's the pipe this project builds: the "pipes that feed leads in" line from a GTM Engineer job description, made real.

Maple also runs live integrations through POS/reservation partners — OpenTable, and POS platforms including Shift4/SkyTab, Quantic, and TRAY, per its own announced partnerships. A restaurant already running one of those partner platforms has less activation friction for a Maple-style agent than one running nothing at all — that distinction is built directly into the scoring model below.

the precision lesson — found twice while building this, not hypothetical

A bare-word grep for "tock" during early manual scoping produced two real false positives on this exact dataset:

  • Tick Tock Diner matched because the restaurant's own name contains "tock" — it actually runs OpenTable, not Tock.
  • samsunnynyc.com also matched "tock" — from a JS config string, "stores.EnableOutOfStockAlignment", nothing to do with reservations at all.

fingerprint.py requires the real domain fragment (exploretock.com, not the word "tock") specifically because of these two catches — both verified clean in the current output. Small example, but it's the actual argument for evidence-based detection over keyword matching.
